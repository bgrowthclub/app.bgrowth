# BGrowth Vision

**BGrowth is the operating system for business growth.**

This document is not a technical specification. It describes the business
— what BGrowth is for, who it serves, where it's going, and how it makes
money. For how the software is built, see ARCHITECTURE.md and CLAUDE.md.

---

## Mission

Most people who start a service business aren't short on effort — they're
short on structure. There is no shortage of advice about starting a
business. There is a shortage of something that turns that advice into
action: the actual steps, in the actual order, ready to work through today.

BGrowth's mission is to give every service professional — the notary, the
cleaner, the bookkeeper, the delivery driver, the handyman, the mobile
detailer — the same thing a franchise gives its owners: a proven,
step-by-step operating system for their business, without the franchise
fees, the territory restrictions, or the years it takes to build that
structure from scratch.

## Vision

A Business System™ for every service profession — chosen, not built from
scratch. Someone deciding to become a notary, or launch a cleaning company,
or start bookkeeping for local businesses, should be able to pick their
profession and find the system already waiting for them: how to launch it,
how to price it, how to run it every day, and how to grow it — organized,
sequenced, and ready to use the same day they decide to start.

BGrowth is not a course platform and it is not a content library in the
traditional sense. **Courses teach. Systems do.** A course explains a
concept once, on video, and leaves the viewer to translate it into practice
alone. A Business System is software the owner works *from* — every single
time they run their business, not once, passively, before forgetting it.

## The BGrowth Ecosystem

BGrowth is a single company built as a connected ecosystem of products.
Each piece serves a different moment in a business owner's journey, and
each is designed to hand off to the next:

- **BGrowth Academy** — the teaching layer. Where a prospective business
  owner learns what a profession actually involves, what it takes to
  succeed at it, and whether it's the right path for them, before they
  commit money or time.

- **BGrowth Library** — the reference layer. Free and premium guides,
  templates, articles, and state-by-state requirements — the "look things
  up" layer that supports every other product, available to anyone whether
  or not they've bought anything yet.

- **BGrowth Club** — the membership layer. A recurring membership that
  unlocks member pricing across every Business System, exclusive resources,
  and priority access to new systems and features as they ship. Club is how
  a customer who's serious about growth engages with BGrowth on an ongoing
  basis, rather than one purchase at a time.

- **BGrowth Find** — the discovery layer. Helping a prospective business
  owner find the right profession, industry, and Business System for their
  situation — matching people to the business that fits them, rather than
  requiring them to already know what they want.

- **BGrowth Marketplace** — the commerce and partner layer. Where Business
  Systems are bought and sold, and where trusted third-party products and
  services (bonding, insurance, equipment, software) are surfaced as
  affiliate and partner offers directly inside the systems that need them.

- **BGrowth App** — the operating layer. The software surface where a
  member actually runs their business day to day: opening a Business
  System, working through its Planners™, Workflows™, and Toolkits™, and
  returning to it as an ongoing operating tool, not a one-time download.
  `app.bgrowth` (this repository) is the current, foundational
  implementation of this layer.

- **BGrowth AI** — the intelligence layer. AI-assisted modules and guidance
  woven into Business Systems themselves — helping a member fill out a
  planner, make a pricing decision, or draft a client communication,
  without needing to leave their system to go ask somewhere else.

- **BGrowth Services** — the done-for-you layer. For members who want part
  of their Business System handled by BGrowth or a vetted partner directly,
  rather than doing it themselves — the bridge from "software that guides
  you" to "work done on your behalf."

Together, these form one growth operating system: **Find** the right
business, **learn** it in the **Academy**, **launch and run** it in the
**App**, **buy and sell** through the **Marketplace**, **look things up**
in the **Library**, get **AI** help along the way, go **deeper** with
**Club** membership, and hand off the parts you don't want to do yourself
to **Services**.

## Business Philosophy

1. **Systems, not courses.** BGrowth does not sell hours of video. It sells
   structure a person works from repeatedly. If a feature only makes sense
   as something a customer watches once, it does not belong in BGrowth's
   core product.
2. **Software you work from, not a document you read.** Every Business
   System runs live in the browser — a member fills it out, prints it, or
   saves a copy — not a static PDF someone has to interpret and adapt
   themselves.
3. **Repeatable over remarkable.** Running a service business is repetitive
   work — the same signing, the same monthly close, the same client
   intake. Business Systems are built to be reused identically every time,
   not to be impressive once.
4. **One system, every profession.** The same underlying system shape
   (a bundle of Planners™, Workflows™, and Toolkits™) should serve a
   notary and a cleaner and a bookkeeper equally well — profession-specific
   content, not profession-specific software.
5. **Trust compounds across the ecosystem.** A member who trusts BGrowth
   enough to buy one Business System should find every adjacent product —
   Club membership, Marketplace partners, AI guidance, done-for-you
   Services — waiting for them inside the same experience, not sold to them
   as a disconnected upsell.

## Customer Journey

1. **Discover** — a prospective owner finds BGrowth through Find, an
   Industry page, or a specific Business System, often searching for their
   exact profession ("start a notary business," "cleaning business
   launch").
2. **Learn** — they explore the Academy and Library to understand the
   profession and confirm it's right for them, at no cost and no
   commitment.
3. **Choose** — they land on a Business System's product page: what it
   includes, what it costs, who it's for, and proof it works (reviews,
   FAQ).
4. **Buy** — they purchase a single system outright (lifetime access, no
   subscription), or join BGrowth Club for member pricing across every
   system plus exclusive resources.
5. **Run** — the system appears in My Systems and they work through it
   module by module — Planners™ to prepare, Workflows™ to operate,
   Toolkits™ to equip — printing or saving results as needed.
6. **Grow** — as their business matures, they return for the next system
   (equipment → operations → specialization), get recommended partner
   products through the Marketplace, and go deeper into Club, AI-assisted
   modules, or done-for-you Services.
7. **Repeat** — because the business itself is repetitive, they keep
   coming back to the same Workflows and Toolkits indefinitely — BGrowth
   becomes the operating layer of their business, not a one-time purchase.

## Revenue Model

- **Individual Business System sales** — one-time purchases, lifetime
  access, no subscription required. The lowest-commitment way to buy.
- **BGrowth Club membership** — recurring revenue in exchange for member
  pricing on every system, exclusive resources, and priority access to new
  systems and features.
- **Affiliate and partner revenue** — commissions from trusted third-party
  products and services (bonding, insurance, equipment, software)
  recommended contextually inside the Business Systems that need them.
- **Marketplace transaction revenue** (future) — as the Marketplace opens
  to more sellers and partners, a share of transactions flowing through it.
- **Services revenue** (future) — done-for-you offerings for members who
  want part of their system handled directly, priced separately from the
  self-serve system itself.
- **Free tier as an acquisition engine** — free Business Systems, free
  Library resources, and the newsletter exist to build trust and audience
  ahead of a purchase, not to monetize directly.

## Long-Term Roadmap

**Phase 1 — Prove the core (current phase).** A single, static,
browser-run Business System Runtime with a real catalog of systems across
a handful of professions, sold individually and through an early Club
offering. This is where `app.bgrowth` is today.

**Phase 2 — Studio & content scale.** BGrowth Studio becomes the internal
tool that lets BGrowth (and eventually partners) author new Business
Systems — Planners, Workflows, Toolkits, Templates — without hand-writing
data files, and export them into the Runtime. Catalog breadth expands
across many more professions.

**Phase 3 — Accounts, persistence, and real commerce.** Real
authentication, saved member progress, real checkout and membership
billing, and a genuine My Systems library replace today's static
placeholders — without changing what a Business System *is* to the member.

**Phase 4 — Ecosystem activation.** Academy, Library, Find, Marketplace,
AI, and Services come online as connected products around the same account
and the same Business System data, each reinforcing the others rather than
operating as separate silos.

**Phase 5 — Platform.** BGrowth Studio and the Marketplace open further,
letting trusted partners and eventually independent creators build and
sell their own Business Systems on top of BGrowth's Runtime, AI, and
distribution — BGrowth becomes the platform other growth products are
built on, not only a product itself.

## Future Platform Evolution

Every product in the ecosystem is designed to plug into the same member,
the same account, and the same catalog of Business Systems — not to be
rebuilt as a separate app with its own login and its own data. As BGrowth
grows, the App remains the place work actually gets done; Academy, Library,
Find, Marketplace, AI, and Services all exist to get the right person, with
the right context, into the right Business System inside the App — and to
support them once they're there.
